## 6. The Four Health Record Types

A core insight from Yajur Healthcare's research is that four types of health records form the foundation of longitudinal healthcare and AI readiness:

1. **Clinical Records** — Doctor visit notes, diagnoses, physical examinations, clinical observations
2. **Lab Tests** — Laboratory results providing biomarkers (blood tests, biochemical state)
3. **Radiology Exams** — Imaging records (X-rays, MRIs, Ultrasounds, CT scans)
4. **Pharmacy Prescriptions** — Medication records showing history of interventions, drug interactions

These four types, aligned with India's **ABHA Health Records framework**, collectively enable:
- Multi-modal Biomedical AI development
- Longitudinal healthcare records
- Precision and recall in diagnostic AI
- Personalized treatment recommendations

In India's context, per Praxis Global Alliance's 2021 report: Indians do 3 doctor consultations/year on average; each leads to one prescription, and a diagnostic test is prescribed every 3.5 consultations.

## 7. Clinical Reasoning Pipelines

Yajur Healthcare's key differentiation is in building **clinical reasoning pipelines** — not just models.

### The Core Problem
The bottleneck in clinical AI is not model intelligence — it's the quality of clinical context:
- Real EHRs are messy: 20+ unstructured elements per encounter
- Models hallucinate when context is missing — dangerous in medicine
- A model trained on academic cases fails in real-world EHR environments

### Yajur's HITL Annotation Pipeline
Yajur operates a **Human-in-the-Loop pipeline** blending:
- Clinical expertise from physicians, radiologists, nurses across specialties
- Structured validation and feedback-aware loops
- India's skilled, multilingual clinical workforce (250,000+ annotation contributors)
- 22 major Indian languages coverage for multilingual clinical NLP

### Context Engineering Stack
- Targeted retrieval and structured summarization
- Token optimization while preserving clinical relevance
- Outputs validated against FHIR and ICD-10
- Every recommendation includes a direct citation trail

### Safety Principles
1. **Schema-aware data validation** — FHIR-compliant structured outputs are non-negotiable
2. **Traceable provenance** — Every output has a source pointer for auditability
3. **Ambiguity surfaces** — Flag uncertainty; escalate instead of silently guessing
4. **Human-in-the-loop as design principle** — Clinician review is the default confirmation step

## 8. Task Framework for Healthcare AI

Yajur published a **Task Framework for Healthcare AI** (March 2025) that maps EHR workflows to task-driven agentic architectures:

### Key Concept
Healthcare systems are inherently non-linear. The shift must be from **transaction-driven to task-driven** EHR models to enable AI agents.

### EHR to Task Mapping
```
Patient Registration (Project Type)
└── Visit (OP, IP, Daycare) (Project Type)
    └── Visit Summary (Task Types by role)
        ├── Visit Orders (doctor)
        ├── Consultation (doctor)
        ├── Laboratory Reports (pathologist)
        ├── Radiology Reports (radiologist)
        ├── Nursing Interventions (nurse)
        └── Discharge Summary (nurse, doctor)
```

### Task States
- **Pending** — Waiting for execution
- **In Progress** — Currently being executed
- **Paused** — On hold due to dependencies
- **Completed** — Successfully finished
- **Failed** — Not completed due to errors

### AI Agent Types (Care Team Swarm)
- Front Desk Agent
- Concierge Agent
- OP Nurse Agent
- Doctor Agent
- Consultant Agent
- **Meta Reviewer Agent** — tracks all activities, generates handover summaries
- Care Plan Author-Reviewer Agent

### Agentic Patterns (maps to Andrew Ng's four patterns)
- **Reflection** → Meta Reviewer Agent (handover summaries)
- **Tool Use** → Task-as-container concept (opens underlying EHR feature via API)
- **Planning** → Care Plan Author Agent (generates task sequences)
- **Multi-Agent Collaboration** → Care Team Swarm (shared patient context)

- **859+ million ABHA accounts** linked to 878+ million health records
- **Federated architecture** — Data stays at originating hospital; interoperability via consistent API layer
- **SAHI** (Strategy for Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare) — India's national AI healthcare governance framework

Key principle: "Recency and relevancy" — patient information must be current and contextually relevant across the care network.

## 12. NHCX — National Health Claims Exchange

**NHCX** is India's government-built interoperability platform for health insurance claims (by National Health Authority).

### Problem It Solves
- Hospital billing teams must log into separate portals for each insurer
- Most claims take 15–30 days; regulatory mandate is 1-hour pre-auth, 3-hour discharge approval
- Manual processes prone to rejection, delays, patient financial stress

### How NHCX Works
**"One rail, many claims"** — like Indian Railways infrastructure:
- Hospital connects once to NHCX
- NHCX routes to all insurers (standardized, encrypted)
- Automated eligibility checks, pre-authorization, claims adjudication, settlement

### Built on
- FHIR R4 standards
- ABDM integration (ABHA IDs for beneficiary validation)
- Structured InsurancePlan API for pre-procedure coverage checks

### Opportunities NHCX Creates
- Automated pre-auth engines
- Claims adjudication co-pilots
- Fraud pattern detection at scale
- Insurance policy digitization
- Real-time claims tracking for patients

**URL:** https://yajur.ai/gcoe-patient-oncocare-v7.html

A precision patient navigation operating system that orchestrates every touchpoint of a cancer patient's journey — from first diagnosis to last follow-up — across a hub-and-spoke care network reaching every pin code.

**What it demonstrates:**

- **Onco-CoE Command Center** — a real-time hospital operations dashboard with a live active patient care board, live alerts, and weekly treatment schedules
- **Orchestrated Patient Flight Plan** — every patient's care mapped as a living, adaptive AI-generated journey, continuously monitored and shifted between hub and spoke as clinical situations evolve
- **Hub & Spoke Care Network** — a central hospital hub connected digitally to partner clinics, nursing centers, diagnostic labs, and pharmacies extending reach to every regional pin code
- **20 Signature AI Innovations** — AI-powered flagship capabilities across clinical, operational, and patient-facing domains
- **Intelligence Fabric** — standards-based interoperability layer (FHIR, ABDM) enabling real-time data flow across the entire network
- **Citizen Longevity Index & Early Detection** — population-health layer with community-led early detection and a Longevity Quotient (LQ) derived from multi-ring biomarkers
- **India's National Cancer Mission alignment** — purpose-built to operationalise GoI health vision into hospital-level digital workflows
- **Three-Tier Oncology Ecosystem** — Apex Cancer Center (national CoE) → Regional Hub → Community Spokes

**Target audience:** Oncology hospitals, cancer centres of excellence, health systems building hub-and-spoke cancer care networks, government health departments.

**Why it matters:** Cancer is India's fastest-growing disease burden. Most hospitals manage cancer patients reactively and in silos. Onco-CoE turns patient navigation into a proactive, AI-orchestrated operation — the same way air traffic control turns individual flights into a coordinated, safe system.
